Badminton

Where does badminton come from?

Badminton has its origins in the following anecdote. It is said that one day in 1873, English officers returning from India were gathered at the Duke of Beaufort's castle in Badminton, in Badminton, an English town in Gloucestershire, came to talk about the Indian game of "poona", which was played with a racket and a light ball. They set out to play it. But as they had no ball at hand they decided to use a champagne cork, to which they attached some feathers. Amused and seduced by their discovery, they decided to make the game known under the name of the castle where it was born: Badminton. Four years later, they published the first rules of the game and took credit for it, although they had not actually invented anything.

Badminton Rules


The rules in simple:

At the beginning of the set and whenever the score is even (0, 2, 4, 6...) the server serves in the right service area and must send it to the opponent's right service area. If the score is odd, the server serves in the left service area. A point is over when the person puts the shuttlecock into the net or past the single side lines or the opponent has dropped the shuttlecock on the floor. If the server wins the rally he/she scores the point and serves into the alternative zone. If the receiver wins the rally, he/she scores a point and serves according to his/her score.
In singles and doubles it is strictly forbidden to serve in the area before the service area if it falls in then the service is lost.


The rules for doubles and mixed doubles:

At the beginning of the set it is the person on the right who serves. As long as he doesn't lose the point he always serves and changes sides. If he loses the point it is his opponent's turn to serve and when it is his team's turn it is the other person on his team who serves.
A point is over when the person puts the shuttlecock into the net or past the double side lines or the opponent has dropped the shuttlecock on the floor.
In doubles it is strictly forbidden to serve in the front line of the service area and strictly forbidden to serve after the long service line. Otherwise, during the rally you can serve in both areas.
